## Term 1
### Lesson 3 - Mindmaps
#### Recall
Students shall be asked to recall:
- The rules of the IT lab
- How to open their OneNote
- How to open File Explorer and navigate to their documents

#### Objective
Students shall be able to describe what a mind map is, why it is used, and how it is used in planning, and shall create a mind map about themselves that begins the "About Me" presentation running through this term's PowerPoint unit.

#### Mindmaps
Students shall record some basic facts about themselves that will form the content of their mind map and, later, their "About Me" PowerPoint:
- Their favourite subject at school, and why
- Their favourite food, and why
- Their favourite movie, and why
- Their favourite things to watch
- A hobby they do or would like to do, and why
- Their favourite thing at school
- Something they are most proud of that they have done in the last few weeks

Students shall be taught:
- What a mind map is and why it is used
- How mind maps are used in planning
- The constituent parts of a mind map: central theme, nodes, sub-nodes

Students shall then create a mind map about themselves, working up through the following levels as time and ability allow:
- Level 1 (Structural): central theme (about you), 5 nodes, one sub-node in each
- Level 2 (Multi-structural): central theme, 5 nodes, 3 sub-nodes in each
- Level 3 (Relational): central theme, 5 nodes, 5 sub-nodes in each
- Level 4 (Extended): central theme, 5 nodes, 5 sub-nodes in each, with at least one sub-node of a sub-node

##### Equipment used
###### Teacher
The teacher shall use the assigned PowerPoint to introduce mind maps and model building one live in the mind-mapping software.
The teacher shall use the screen to demonstrate adding a central theme, nodes and sub-nodes.
###### Student
Each student shall use the class mind-mapping software on their own PC to record their "About Me" facts and build their mind map.
