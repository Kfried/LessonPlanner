## Term 1
### Lesson 4 - Introduction to PowerPoint and Outline View Mode
#### Recall
Students shall be asked to recall:
- What a mind map is
- What a mind map is used for
- The different parts of a mind map (central theme, nodes, sub-nodes)

#### Objective
Students shall be able to describe why PowerPoint is used, open and save a new PowerPoint file, describe what outline view is and what it is used for, and demonstrate that they can transfer the information from their "About Me" mind map into outline view.

#### Introduction to PowerPoint
Students shall be taught:
- Why we use PowerPoint
- How to open a new PowerPoint file
- How to save a PowerPoint file to their OneDrive
- What outline view is and what it is used for

Students shall then practise the skill of using outline view: they shall open a new PowerPoint, switch to outline view, and transfer the branches from their "About Me" mind map into the outline as slide titles and bullet points, so that their mind map becomes the structural plan for their presentation.

##### Equipment used
###### Teacher
The teacher shall use the assigned PowerPoint to introduce outline view and model transferring a sample mind map into it.
The teacher shall use the screen to demonstrate opening, saving and navigating outline view.
###### Student
Each student shall use PowerPoint on their own PC, along with their completed mind map from the previous lesson, to build their outline.
