## Term 1
### Lesson 10 - How to Present and Deliver your PowerPoint
#### Recall
Students shall be asked to recall:
- That a PowerPoint can be automated
- The correct settings for a timed, self-running presentation

#### Objective
Students shall know the different techniques used to deliver a presentation, understand and evaluate what techniques make for a good presentation, and practise the skill of presenting by delivering their completed "About Me" PowerPoint to their class.

#### How to Present and Deliver your PowerPoint
Students shall be taught:
- Techniques used to deliver a presentation effectively (for example: eye contact, pace, projecting your voice, not reading directly from the slide, structuring an introduction and conclusion)
- How to evaluate what makes a presentation good, using agreed success criteria

Students shall then practise the skill of presenting: each student shall deliver their completed "About Me" PowerPoint, built up over this term's lessons, to the class (or a small group, depending on time and confidence), and shall give and receive constructive peer feedback against the agreed success criteria.

##### Equipment used
###### Teacher
The teacher shall use the assigned PowerPoint to introduce presentation delivery techniques and model presenting confidently using a worked example.
The teacher shall use the screen/projector and a peer-feedback checklist to structure the presenting and feedback rounds.
###### Student
Each student shall use their own completed "About Me" PowerPoint, presented from their PC or the front-of-room screen, to deliver their presentation to the class and give peer feedback using the checklist.
