# Year 7 Computing Scheme of Work

## Curriculum Intent

The Year 7 Computing curriculum develops students' confidence as capable and responsible users of digital technology. Through Microsoft Office applications, digital literacy and online safety, students develop the foundational knowledge and skills needed for future study in Computing. Throughout every unit, students are encouraged to apply the core strands of Computational Thinking and Problem Solving. These are embedded through activities that require decomposition, logical reasoning, pattern recognition, abstraction, evaluation and iterative improvement. Students are taught to approach tasks methodically, evaluate outcomes and refine solutions, regardless of the software or digital tool being used.

## Core Curriculum Threads

The following strands are developed continuously throughout the year:

- Computational Thinking
- Problem Solving
- Logical Reasoning
- Creativity
- Independence
- Evaluation and Reflection
- Digital Literacy
- Digital Citizenship

## Autumn Term 1 - Microsoft PowerPoint & NGRT Assessment

### Learning Objectives

Students will:

- Complete the NGRT Reading Assessment.
- Navigate PowerPoint confidently.
- Plan presentations for different audiences.
- Apply effective design principles.
- Organise information logically.
- Select appropriate media.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students develop computational thinking by:

- Breaking presentation tasks into manageable stages (decomposition).
- Planning logical sequences of slides.
- Evaluating different approaches to communicating information.
- Refining solutions based on feedback.

### Problem Solving

Students solve design challenges by selecting appropriate layouts, organising information clearly and improving presentations through evaluation and iteration.

## Autumn Term 2 - Presentation Skills

### Learning Objectives

- Apply consistent house style.
- Manipulate images effectively.
- Use animations purposefully.
- Refine presentations through peer and self-evaluation.
- Present information effectively for different audiences.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students:

- Evaluate multiple design solutions.
- Identify patterns within effective presentations.
- Apply abstraction by focusing on essential information.
- Refine digital products through iterative improvement.

### Problem Solving

Students justify design choices, test presentation effectiveness and adapt their work based on feedback.

### Lesson Journey

#### 1: Mindmaps

**Objectives:**

- Know what a mindmap is and what a good one looks like
- Understand how a mindmap can be used as a planning tool
- Practice the skill of creating a mindmap

#### 2: Introduction to Powerpoint and Outline View Mode

**Objectives:**

- Know what how to create a new powerpoint in outline view mode
- Understand the benefits of using outline view
- Practice the skill of making a powerpoint in outline view

#### 3: Adding Images and Image Manipulation

**Objectives:**

- Know how to collect copyright free images online for use in other files
- Understand the variety of manipulation techniques available in powerpoint
- Practice the skill of manipulating images and putting into powerpoint

#### 4: Styles and Themes (transitions, animations, Design)

**Objectives:**

- Know where to locate the options for styles, themes and animations
- Understand the effect of applying these tools to a powerpoint
- Practice the skill of applying styles and themes

#### 5: Adding Sound and Video

**Objectives:**

- Know where to locate the options for sound and video clips
- Understand the different audio and video options within powerpoint
- Practice the skill of applying adding video and audio content

#### 6: URLs (internal and external)

**Objectives:**

- Know what a URL is and how to embed in a powerpoint
- Understand the difference between Internal and External URLs
- Practice the skill of adding URLs to a powerpoint

#### 7: Automating Your Powerpoint and Final Improvements

**Objectives:**

- Know that powerpoints can be automated
- Understand how to apply the correct settings for timed presentations
- Practice the skill of creating automated powerpoints

#### 8: How to Present and Delivering your Powerpoint

**Objectives:**

- Know the different techniques used to deliver a presentation
- Understand and evaluate what techniques make for a good presentation
- Practice the skill of presenting

## Spring Term 1 - Microsoft Excel Fundamentals

### Learning Objectives

- Enter and organise data.
- Format spreadsheets effectively.
- Write simple formulae.
- Use cell references accurately.
- Interpret spreadsheet outputs.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students:

- Recognise patterns in numerical data.
- Build logical sequences using formulae.
- Develop precision through structured problem solving.
- Understand spreadsheets as automated solutions.

### Problem Solving

Students identify efficient methods for performing calculations and evaluate whether spreadsheet outputs meet intended outcomes.

## Spring Term 2 - Spreadsheet Modelling

### Learning Objectives

- Use built-in functions.
- Apply IF statements.
- Create charts.
- Analyse data.
- Develop spreadsheet models.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students:

- Apply algorithmic thinking through formula construction.
- Use abstraction to simplify complex calculations.
- Test and debug spreadsheet models.
- Evaluate the efficiency of different solutions.

### Problem Solving

Students design spreadsheet models that solve authentic problems, testing and refining their work until it produces reliable results.

### Lesson Journey

#### 1: Introduction to Spreadsheets and Layout

**Objectives:**
- Know what a spreadsheet is and identify the main features of the Excel interface.
- Understand the purpose of rows, columns, cells and worksheets.
- Practice the skill of navigating Excel and entering basic data.

#### 2: Borders and Fills

**Objectives:**
- Know where to find the formatting tools for borders, fills and fonts.
- Understand how formatting can improve the appearance and readability of a spreadsheet.
- Practice the skill of applying borders, colours and formatting to spreadsheet data.

#### 3: Calculations and Functions

**Objectives:**
- Know how to create basic formulae and use common functions such as SUM, AVERAGE, MIN and MAX.
- Understand the difference between formulae and functions and when to use each.
- Practice the skill of creating calculations using cell references.

#### 4: COUNTIF

**Objectives:**
- Know how to use the COUNTIF function.
- Understand how COUNTIF can be used to count cells that meet specific criteria.
- Practice the skill of creating COUNTIF functions using different conditions.

#### 5: Relative and Absolute Cell References

**Objectives:**
- Know the difference between relative and absolute cell references.
- Understand when absolute references should be used within formulae.
- Practice the skill of creating and copying formulae using both relative and absolute references.

#### 6: Conditional Models

**Objectives:**
- Know how to create simple IF statements in Excel.
- Understand how conditional formulae can be used to automate decisions.
- Practice the skill of creating spreadsheets using IF functions to produce different outcomes.

#### 7: More Conditional Functions

**Objectives:**
- Know how to use more advanced conditional functions such as nested IF statements and AND/OR functions.
- Understand how multiple conditions can be combined to solve more complex problems.
- Practice the skill of creating spreadsheets using advanced conditional formulae.

### Learning Objectives

- Research effectively online.
- Identify misinformation.
- Recognise bias.
- Understand digital footprints.
- Use social media responsibly.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students:

- Evaluate information critically.
- Identify patterns in trustworthy sources.
- Apply logical reasoning when judging online content.
- Make evidence-based decisions.

### Problem Solving

Students investigate online scenarios, considering risks and selecting appropriate responses to real-world situations.

## Summer Term 2 - Responsible Digital Media

### Learning Objectives

- Understand copyright.
- Reference digital assets appropriately.
- Edit multimedia responsibly.
- Reflect on digital behaviour.
- Demonstrate safe online practices.

### Computational Thinking Focus

Students:

- Evaluate digital resources.
- Select appropriate media.
- Refine multimedia products.
- Reflect on digital decisions.

### Problem Solving

Students complete a digital media project that requires planning, evaluation and iterative improvement while demonstrating responsible digital citizenship.

#### 1: Introduction to eSafety

**Objectives:**
- Know what eSafety is.
- Understand the risks associated with using the internet.
- Practice the skill of evaluating online risks to decide on the best preventative measures.

#### 2: Staying Safe Online

**Objectives:**
- Know what to do in a dangerous eSafety scenario.
- Understand the different types of online risks and compare appropriate responses.
- Practice the skill of staying safe online by offering advice to others.

#### 3: Social Media Safety

**Objectives:**
- Know how social media can be dangerous.
- Understand the actions that can make social media use unsafe.
- Practice the skill of creating media assets to promote eSafety awareness.

#### 4: Storyboarding and Planning an eSafety Campaign

**Objectives:**
- Know what a storyboard is and how it is used in media.
- Understand how to use a storyboard to plan an effective eSafety message.
- Practice the skill of creating a storyboard suitable for the intended audience.

#### 5: Creating an eSafety Video

**Objectives:**
- Know the different types of assets that can be used in a video.
- Understand how to sequence media assets to tell a clear and engaging story.
- Practice the skill of creating an eSafety video using video editing software.
